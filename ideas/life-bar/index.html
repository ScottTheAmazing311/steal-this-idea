LifeBar

Your daily supplements, served fresh.

A walk-in supplement bar where vitamins, minerals, and adaptogens are served as delicious, precisely dosed shots and drinks. Think of it as a coffee shop, but instead of caffeine, you are powering up with creatine, magnesium, omega-3s, and D3, all in forms that actually taste good.

The Problem

We know what we need. We just hate how we get it.

Most people know they should be taking supplements like magnesium, vitamin D, omega-3s, and creatine. But the experience is terrible. Chalky powders you mix into water and force down. Horse-pill capsules that are hard to swallow. Confusing dosages on bottles with tiny print. And then there is the biggest problem of all: the bottles just sit in your cabinet, forgotten. People want to be healthier. The delivery mechanism is what is broken.

The Solution

Walk in. Power up. Feel incredible.

LifeBar serves supplements as crafted, great-tasting shots and small drinks. Walk in, tell the bar staff your goals or health profile, and get a personalized flight of wellness shots. Each one is precisely dosed, flavor-optimized, and served fresh. Your creatine tastes like a citrus spritz. Your magnesium comes in a lavender-honey blend. Your omega-3 is a smooth mango shot. It is the ritual of a coffee shop combined with the function of a pharmacy, without the sterile clinical feel of either.

The Opportunity

A $55 Billion Market, Served Poorly

The U.S. dietary supplement market is valued at over $55 billion and growing 7% annually. The juice and smoothie bar market adds another $3 billion. LifeBar sits at the intersection, targeting the growing segment of consumers who want functional nutrition in a convenient, social format. There is also an underserved market for a "third place" that is not a coffee shop or a bar.

Business Plan

From flagship bar to personalized wellness brand

Revenue Model

Per-visit sales of individual shots ($4-8 each) and curated flights ($12-20). Subscription memberships for daily regulars at a discount. Branded take-home shot packs for weekly use. Custom supplement profiles with personalized formulations at a premium tier. The subscription model creates predictable recurring revenue once customers find their routine.

Go-to-Market Strategy

Open a flagship location in a health-forward urban market like Austin, Portland, Salt Lake City, or Brooklyn. Partner with local gyms and fitness studios for cross-promotions. Build a strong social media presence around the "shot flight" visual aesthetic, which is inherently shareable. Offer first-visit free tastings to drive trial and lower the barrier to entry for supplement newcomers.

Competitive Advantage

While juice bars and smoothie shops exist, none focus specifically on supplement delivery as the core product. LifeBar owns the intersection of precise nutrition and great taste. The personalization engine and subscription model create sticky, recurring customers that generic juice bars cannot match. The shot-based format is also novel and social-media friendly, driving organic awareness.

Year One Milestones

  • Open flagship location and serve 200+ customers per day within six months
  • Launch subscription membership program with 500+ recurring members
  • Develop and release a branded take-home shot pack line in 10 formulations

Startup Cost Estimate

A single flagship location with bar buildout, initial inventory, and branding.

$150,000 - $400,000

Key Risks

  • Supplement bars and juice bars already exist in major coastal cities; differentiation must be clear and consistent
  • FDA regulations around supplement claims and food service add compliance complexity that requires legal guidance
  • Customer education is required to shift people from pill-based supplement habits to a walk-in bar format
