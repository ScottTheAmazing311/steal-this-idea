The Problem

Waiting Shouldn't Feel Like Suffering

Whether you are recovering from surgery, waiting on a delayed flight, or stuck in a budget hotel, the experience is almost always the same: uncomfortable, boring, and frustrating.

Rock-Hard Pillows, Paper-Thin Blankets

Hospital bedding is designed for sanitation, not sleep. Airport benches are steel. Hotel pillows feel like they were packed in 1998.

Nothing to Watch, Nothing to Do

Basic cable with 12 channels. Spotty Wi-Fi. No preloaded entertainment. Hours of staring at a ceiling or a departure board.

No Good Way to Fix It

Gift shops sell overpriced keychains. You cannot rent a Switch at the hospital. There is no comfort concierge at any budget hotel.

The Solution

Rent Comfort in Three Simple Steps

Comfy House installs rental kiosks in hospitals, airports, hotels, and waiting areas. Pick a kit, enjoy your stay, return it when you leave.

1

Scan at the Kiosk

Walk up to any Comfy House kiosk, tap your phone or credit card, and browse available comfort and entertainment packages.

2

Pick Your Kit

Choose a cozy comfort pack with blankets and pillows, or go premium with a loaded tablet, gaming console, and noise-canceling headphones.

3

Return and Relax

When your stay is over, drop everything at the kiosk or nurses station. We handle sanitization and restocking.

The Business Plan

Built to Scale, Designed to Delight

Revenue Model

Tiered per-stay rental fees. A basic comfort pack (blanket, pillow, slippers) runs around $20. Premium entertainment bundles with tablets, consoles, and headphones go for $40 to $50. Revenue sharing agreements with venue partners provide placement access and mutual upside.

Go-to-Market Strategy

Launch with a pilot program in 3 to 5 hospitals in one metro area, starting with maternity wards where stays are predictable and visitors are highly motivated. Use patient satisfaction data and success metrics to pitch larger hospital networks for broader rollout.

Competitive Advantage

No one currently serves the comfort rental niche in institutional waiting environments. Hospital gift shops sell overpriced trinkets instead of useful comfort items. Comfy House replaces that dead retail space with a high-margin rental model that solves a universal problem.

Year One Milestones

  • Secure pilot agreements with 5 hospitals and deploy kiosks in maternity and long-stay wards
  • Reach 1,000 rentals per month with a 90%+ return rate and strong patient feedback
  • Expand to 2 airport locations and begin franchise model development

Key Risks

  • Hospitals may resist due to infection control policies and liability concerns around third-party items
  • Theft and damage rates on electronics could erode margins if deposit systems are not well designed
  • Venue partnerships require long sales cycles with institutional decision makers

Startup Cost Estimate

Covers kiosk hardware, initial inventory (blankets, pillows, tablets, consoles), sanitization equipment, venue partnership development, and working capital for the first 6 months of operations.
