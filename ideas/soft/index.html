The Problem

Softness is the most desired quality. And the hardest to shop for.

You order a blanket online and hope for the best. You squeeze a pillow through plastic packaging at a store and guess. You touch a rug sample and wonder if the full-size version will feel the same. Softness is one of the most universally wanted qualities in home goods, bedding, and clothing. Yet no retailer curates specifically for it. Bed Bath and Beyond is gone. The void it left behind is still waiting to be filled by something better.

The Solution

One store. One rule. Everything is soft.

Soft is a retail experience, both in-store and online, where every product has been hand-selected for maximum softness. Walk in and touch everything. Feel the blankets, squeeze the pillows, run your hand across the rugs. Every listing on the website carries a verified softness standard. No guessing. No disappointing deliveries. No returns. Whether you are shopping for yourself, a baby shower, a wedding gift, or just because you deserve something cozy, Soft is your destination.

Business Plan

From curation to creation: the Soft playbook

Revenue Model

Phase one is a curation model: sourcing the softest products from partner brands and selling them at retail markup. Phase two introduces private-label Soft branded products, pushing margins to 60-70%. Gift bundles and subscription boxes create recurring revenue. Online sales expand reach beyond the flagship store footprint.

Go-to-Market Strategy

Launch as a direct-to-consumer e-commerce brand with a curated selection of 50-100 products. Build an Instagram-first marketing strategy showcasing the tactile, cozy brand aesthetic. Open a single flagship retail location in a high-traffic shopping district to validate the in-store experience. Partner with influencers in home decor and lifestyle who align with the brand's sensory-first philosophy.

Competitive Advantage

No retailer owns the "soft" positioning. By building a brand entirely around one sensory quality, Soft becomes the default destination for anyone shopping by feel. The curation model starts lean, scales into private label, and the brand itself becomes the moat. Competitors sell products; Soft sells a feeling.

Year One Milestones

  • Launch e-commerce store with 75+ curated products and achieve $500K in first-year revenue
  • Open one flagship retail location and validate the in-store soft experience concept
  • Introduce the first private-label Soft branded product line: blankets and pillows

Key Risks

  • Softness is subjective; maintaining a consistent quality bar as the catalog grows is a real challenge
  • Competing with established home goods retailers like Target and Crate and Barrel on price and selection
  • Brick-and-mortar retail carries significant overhead, and the in-store experience is core to the brand promise
