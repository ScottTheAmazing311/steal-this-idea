The Problem

Backpacks Are Broken

You carry a backpack every single day. It is one of the most-used objects in your life. And yet, most people grab whatever is cheapest at the store, inherit a leftover college bag, or buy an overengineered travel pack with fifteen compartments they will never touch.

The working professional has been completely ignored. There are great hiking packs, great travel packs, great camera bags. But nobody is designing a thoughtful daily carry for the person who just needs their laptop, lunch, sunglasses, and keys organized without the clutter.

The Over-Builder

Locking zippers, four water bottle slots, two sunglasses pockets, a shoe compartment. Built for a trip you are never taking.

The Try-Hard

All-leather, zero compartments, looks like a briefcase strapped to your back. Costs $400 and holds almost nothing.

The Afterthought

Free conference swag, a ten-year-old JanSport, or a generic Best Buy special. Functional, barely. Thoughtful, never.

The Solution

Your Pack. Your Rules.

LastPack is a platform, not just a product. Every backpack starts with a frame: pick your exterior style, from minimal canvas rollup to structured urban shell. Inside, a universal grid rail system lets you snap in modules wherever you want them.

Need a cooled lunch compartment? Snap it in. Prefer a dedicated charger pocket instead? Swap it out. Your backpack evolves alongside your routine, your job, your season. No more dead compartments. No more compromise.

Features

Built Different. On Purpose.

Modular Grid System

Snap-in compartments lock onto a standardized rail. Build from 15+ modules to create the exact internal layout you need. Reconfigure in under a minute.

Multiple Frame Styles

From minimal canvas rollups to structured urban shells to premium leather fronts. Same internal platform, completely different look. Your style, your call.

Swappable Straps

Detachable strap system with multiple comfort levels. Choose slim minimal straps for short commutes or cushioned ergonomic straps for heavier loads.

Universal Laptop Sleeve

Every frame includes a dedicated padded laptop compartment against your back. Fits 13 to 16 inch devices with a secure magnetic closure.

Business Plan

The Blueprint

Revenue Model

Direct-to-consumer e-commerce. Base frames priced at $129 to $189 depending on style. Individual modules from $15 to $45. Starter bundles (frame + 4 to 6 modules) at $199 to $279. Recurring revenue through seasonal module drops and limited-edition frame collaborations.

Go-to-Market Strategy

Launch on Kickstarter with three signature frame styles and 12 initial modules. Partner with productivity YouTubers and commuter lifestyle bloggers for early buzz. Follow up with a DTC Shopify store and pop-up activations in co-working spaces across major metro areas.

Competitive Advantage

While competitors like Away and Timbuk2 offer surface-level customization (colors, flaps), LastPack is the only system where the internal architecture is fully reconfigurable. The standardized grid rail creates a platform ecosystem, driving repeat module purchases and deep brand loyalty.

Year One Milestones

  • Raise $250K+ on Kickstarter and deliver first 2,000 units within 6 months
  • Launch DTC store with 3 frame styles and 15 module SKUs
  • Secure placement in 2 to 3 retail partners (e.g., REI, The Container Store)

Key Risks

  • Engineering a durable yet lightweight modular rail system at a consumer-friendly price
  • High competition and crowdfunding fatigue in the backpack category
  • Educating buyers on the modular concept when most people impulse-buy backpacks
