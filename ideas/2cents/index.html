Business Plan

The Roadmap to
Building 2Cents

Revenue Model

Per-question micro-transactions at $1 each, with an optional unlimited plan at $10-15/month for power users. The platform takes a percentage of each transaction. Advisors earn 10-30 cents per answered question based on their rating and certification level, plus optional tips from satisfied users.

Go-to-Market Strategy

Launch with a single vertical: home improvement. Partner with DIY influencers and home project creators to seed the advisor pool. Once the home improvement community is thriving, expand into cooking, then auto, then travel. Survey existing advisors to identify which categories to unlock next, growing supply organically.

Competitive Advantage

Unlike JustAnswer, which targets expensive professional consultations, 2Cents democratizes advice by tapping into everyday expertise at micro-transaction prices. The low barrier to both asking and answering creates a network effect that professional-only platforms cannot replicate. Categories grow organically as advisors add their skills.

Year One Milestones

  • Launch home improvement vertical with 500 seeded advisors and achieve sub-60-second average response times
  • Reach 50,000 monthly active users across home improvement and cooking categories
  • Hit $500K in annualized gross transaction volume with a 30% platform take rate

Key Risks

Cold Start Problem

The app is only useful if enough advisors are online to answer quickly, requiring significant upfront investment in seeding the community.

Quality Control

Ensuring advice is accurate and safe, especially for topics like home electrical or structural projects where bad advice could cause real harm.

Advisor Retention

Micro-payments may not sustain motivation long-term without strong gamification, community features, and a rewarding user experience.

Estimated Startup Cost
$150K - $400K

To build the MVP, seed the initial advisor community, and launch the first vertical with marketing support.
