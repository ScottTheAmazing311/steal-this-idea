The Problem

You keep saying "I should learn that."
But you never do.

It is not a lack of desire. It is a lack of a clear, simple starting point.

  • Too many options, not enough direction. YouTube has 10,000 watercolor tutorials. Which one is actually good? The search itself becomes the barrier.
  • Getting the right materials is a chore. What brush? What paper? What brand? The research alone kills your momentum before you even begin.
  • Digital-only courses lack commitment. At $9.99/month for unlimited classes, there is no skin in the game. If you learn nothing, you lose nothing.
  • The hobby dream stays a dream. Surveys show most adults have 3+ skills they want to learn but never start. The gap between wanting and doing is where excitement goes to die.
The Solution

One box. One skill. One month.
Everything included.

SkillPost takes the best of MasterClass-level instruction and pairs it with a physical box of everything you need. No research required. No shopping trips. No guessing if you bought the right materials.

"It is like HelloFresh, but for hobbies. They did not just send recipes. They sent the ingredients, the steps, the photos. That changed cooking forever. We are doing the same for every other skill."

Each quarterly box ships with beginner-quality materials, a curated step-by-step video course, and one achievable goal you can hit in 30 days. Learn a harmonica song. Paint a landscape watercolor. Write your name in calligraphy. The goal is small enough to finish, but meaningful enough to hook you.

The Business Plan

How SkillPost becomes a business.

A clear path from first box to sustainable company.

SkillPost Business Plan

Concept from the Steal This Idea podcast

Revenue Model

  • Quarterly subscription boxes at $40 to $60 per box, with tiered pricing based on material cost
  • Premium tier with live one-on-one video tutoring sessions for an additional monthly fee
  • Affiliate revenue from upselling full equipment sets and advanced courses from partner brands
  • Sponsored product placement inside boxes from tool and supply companies eager to reach engaged hobbyists

Go-to-Market Strategy

  • Launch with 5 to 8 curated skill boxes: ukulele, watercolor, calligraphy, harmonica, knitting, charcoal drawing
  • Direct-to-consumer Shopify store with a strong unboxing-focused social presence
  • Partner with lifestyle influencers and target gift-guide placements in major publications
  • Revenue-share model with instructors to minimize upfront content production costs

Competitive Advantage

Unlike Skillshare or YouTube, SkillPost combines physical materials with consistently high-quality instruction in a single package. The tangible box creates commitment that digital-only platforms cannot replicate. This is the same insight that made HelloFresh outperform recipe apps: people follow through when the ingredients are already on the counter.

Startup Cost Estimate

Estimated range to MVP: $50,000 to $150,000. This covers initial inventory for 5+ skill categories, video production for launch courses, website and e-commerce setup, packaging design, and initial marketing spend.

Year One Milestones

  • Launch with 8 skill categories and reach 2,000 active subscribers within the first 6 months
  • Achieve a 70% subscriber retention rate quarter over quarter
  • Introduce the premium tutoring tier and generate 20% of total revenue from upsells and affiliate partnerships

Key Risks

  • Margin pressure: High per-box cost of physical goods and shipping could squeeze margins, especially at lower price tiers
  • Content investment: Each new skill category requires significant upfront video production and instructor partnerships
  • Subscriber churn: Novelty could wear off over time if box quality or skill variety becomes repetitive
