THE PROBLEM

Healthy fast food is still an oxymoron.

You are hungry, short on time, and sitting in your car. Your options? A soggy side salad from a burger chain, or parking, walking in, and waiting 20 minutes at a sit-down restaurant. For anyone who wants something fresh and fast, the gap in the market is painfully obvious.


Salads worth eating require a time commitment. Drive-thru salads require lowering your standards. Until now, there has been no middle ground.

THE SOLUTION

Six salads. Perfected. Ready in two minutes.

FGS keeps it radically simple. We tested over a thousand salad combinations and narrowed it down to six that people genuinely crave. Every ingredient is prepped fresh daily. Every salad is a complete, finished recipe; no building, no customizing, no decision fatigue.


Pull up to the drive-thru, order by number, and you are back on the road before the song on your radio ends. It is the speed of fast food with the quality of a great restaurant.

THE BLUEPRINT

Business Plan

A closer look at how FGS gets from concept to franchise.

Revenue Model

Per-unit salad sales at $12 to $16 per salad with high margins thanks to the limited menu and streamlined prep. Additional revenue from fresh-squeezed lemonades, seasonal soups, and branded dressings sold retail. Franchise fees follow once the model is proven across 3 to 5 company-owned locations.

Go-to-Market Strategy

Launch with a single flagship location in a high-traffic suburban commercial corridor. Build buzz through local food influencer partnerships and a bold, recognizable brand identity. Offer a free salad to every customer during opening week to generate word-of-mouth and repeat visits.

Competitive Advantage

The radically simple menu means faster prep, less food waste, and unmatched consistency. Unlike Sweetgreen or Chopt, FGS does not ask customers to build anything. Every salad is a finished, tested recipe. The drive-thru format removes the biggest friction point in healthy fast food: having to park and walk inside.

Year One Milestones

  • Open flagship drive-thru location and reach 300 orders per day within 6 months
  • Achieve an average ticket of $15 with 65% gross margin on food costs
  • Begin site selection for second and third locations based on proven unit economics

Startup Cost Estimate

Estimated $250,000 to $500,000 for a single drive-thru location, covering build-out, commercial kitchen equipment, initial inventory, branding, and working capital for the first six months of operations.


$250K - $500K TO LAUNCH

Key Risks

  • Perishable inventory and food waste if demand forecasting is off during early operations
  • Consumer perception that salads are not worth paying $12 to $16 for at a drive-thru window
  • Competition from established fast-casual chains adding drive-thru salad options to their menus
