Business Plan

The blueprint for Kitted

Revenue Model

Direct-to-consumer e-commerce with outfit bundles priced at $150 to $350 per kit. Margins of 40 to 60 percent on curated bundles. Additional revenue from seasonal limited-edition collections, influencer collaboration lines, and smart cart cross-selling that recommends mix-and-match pieces across outfits.

Go-to-Market Strategy

Launch with a capsule collection of 5 to 10 complete outfits for fall, targeting men 25 to 40 through Instagram and TikTok ads featuring styled lifestyle photography. Partner with mid-tier fashion influencers who can demonstrate the one-click concept. Start lean on Shopify and scale based on early traction and customer feedback.

Competitive Advantage

Unlike Stitch Fix or Trunk Club, Kitted puts the customer in full control. No surprise boxes, no algorithms guessing your taste. You see exactly what you get before you buy. Unlike Buckle or traditional retailers, you never add items individually or re-enter sizes. The entire experience is purpose-built around complete outfits, not individual garments.

Key Risks

  • Inventory complexity: stocking every item in multiple sizes across many outfits creates warehousing challenges that require careful planning
  • Return rates: bundled outfits carry higher average order values, which may increase returns if fit is inconsistent across brands
  • Customer acquisition: competing for attention in the crowded online menswear space demands significant and creative marketing investment

Year One Milestones

Q1 to Q2

Launch with 10 curated outfits across 3 seasonal collections. Achieve 500 orders in the first quarter and validate product-market fit.

Q2 to Q3

Build a community of 10,000 email subscribers and establish 5 influencer partnerships to drive organic growth and brand awareness.

Q3 to Q4

Reach $500K in gross revenue and expand to 30 outfit options with mix-and-match recommendations powered by the smart cart.

Estimated Startup Cost: $50,000 to $150,000
