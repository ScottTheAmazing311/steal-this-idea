Andre's Kitchen

Dinner, prepped. Just pop it in.

Andre's Kitchen is a drive-through storefront where you pick up fully prepped sheet pan meals ready to bake at home. Call ahead, preheat your oven, grab your meal, and have a hot home-cooked dinner on the table in 30 minutes. No grocery runs, no meal kits, no fast food guilt.

The Problem

It's 6 PM. What's for dinner?

By 6 PM, busy families face an impossible choice: expensive sit-down restaurants with long waits, unhealthy fast food, or the exhausting process of grocery shopping and cooking from scratch. Meal kit services like Blue Apron still require significant prep time and come with excessive packaging and shipping delays.

Restaurants

$60+ for a family, 45-minute waits, tip on top. Every weeknight? Unsustainable.

Fast Food

Quick and cheap, but loaded with guilt. You know it's not real dinner.

Meal Kits

Still 45 minutes of chopping and measuring. Shipped in ice packs. Not fresh.

Cook from Scratch

Grocery run, recipe search, prep, cook, clean. Who has 90 minutes on a Tuesday?

The Solution

Dinner in three steps.

Andre's Kitchen operates small, efficient storefronts where fresh sheet pan meals are prepped all day. Customers call ahead, preheat their oven on the way, pick up a ready-to-bake tray with protein, vegetables, and sides, and have dinner ready minutes after walking in the door. No cooking skill required.

1

Call & Preheat

Browse today's menu, place your order, and preheat your oven to 350 on your way out the door.

2

Drive & Grab

Swing by our storefront on your commute. Your sheet pan meal is fresh, prepped, and waiting.

3

Bake & Enjoy

Pop the tray in the oven. In 25 minutes, sit down to a hot, home-baked dinner with your family.

Key Features

Built for real weeknights.

Ready to Bake in 20-30 Min

Every meal is a complete sheet pan: protein, vegetables, and sides, fully prepped and seasoned. Just slide it in the oven.

Rotating Weekly Menu

Four protein options, two veggie choices, and rotating sides every week. Enough variety to never get bored, curated enough to keep things simple.

Drive-Through Pickup

Call ahead, we prep it, you grab it. In and out in under 2 minutes. Fits right into your commute home.

Locally Sourced & Fresh

No vacuum sealing, no ice packs, no shipping delays. Ingredients are sourced locally and prepped the same day you pick them up.

Market Opportunity

A massive gap between meal kits and fast food.

$60B+

The U.S. meal kit market is $10B+ and the fast-casual dining market exceeds $50B. Andre's Kitchen sits at the intersection, targeting the massive underserved segment of families who want convenience and quality.

Who We Serve

  • Anyone facing the nightly "what's for dinner?" panic
Business Plan

How Andre's Kitchen becomes a business.

Revenue Model

Simple, High-Margin Pricing

Per-meal pricing at $25 to $40 per family tray, beating restaurant prices while maintaining healthy margins on bulk-prepped ingredients. Rotating weekly menus with 4 protein options drive repeat visits. Potential for multi-meal bundles and loyalty programs.

Go-to-Market

Suburban, Commuter-Focused Launch

Launch a single storefront in a suburban area with high family density near commuter routes. Start with dinner-only service to keep operations lean. Build word-of-mouth through local social media, neighborhood Facebook groups, and a weekly menu reveal that creates anticipation.

Competitive Advantage

The Best of Every World

Unlike meal kits, there's zero prep work and ingredients are fresh (not shipped). Unlike fast food, the meals are healthy and home-baked. The Papa Murphy's model proves consumers will pick up food to finish at home, and the tiny Crumbl-style storefront footprint keeps overhead extremely low. You're running a prep kitchen, not a restaurant: minimal staff, no dining room, no servers, no dishwashers.

Year One Milestones

The First 12 Months

  • Open first location and reach 100 meals sold per day within 6 months
  • Achieve a 40% repeat customer rate through the rotating menu model
  • Validate unit economics and begin scouting second location by month 10
Startup Cost
$80K - $150K

Covers a small commercial kitchen lease, prep equipment, initial inventory, and branding. The compact footprint and prep-only operations keep startup costs a fraction of a traditional restaurant.

Key Risks

Challenges to Navigate

  • Finding the right price point that feels fair versus grocery store or takeout alternatives
  • Food safety compliance for selling uncooked or partially prepared proteins to consumers
  • Building habitual behavior, getting customers to think of Andre's Kitchen before defaulting to fast food
