From seed to scale.

A clear path from flagship location to a network of Orchard destinations across the country.

Revenue Model

Multiple revenue streams ensure year-round viability. Tiered admission tickets range from $15 to $45 per person depending on experience level. Add-on workshops (pie baking, cider pressing) generate $20 to $35 each. Retail sales of fresh apples, cider, pies, and branded merchandise provide high-margin supplemental income. Private event bookings for birthdays and corporate outings fill weekday and off-peak capacity. Seasonal pop-up cider stands at partner locations extend reach beyond the main venue.

Go-to-Market Strategy

Launch a flagship location near a major metro area in a region with strong fall culture, such as the Northeast or Mountain West. Build pre-launch buzz through Instagram and TikTok content featuring the Leaf Pit attraction. Partner with local schools for field trip programs and offer early-bird season passes to build a loyal visitor base before opening weekend.

Competitive Advantage

Unlike generic pumpkin patches and corn mazes, The Orchard builds an entire brand ecosystem around a single beloved ingredient. The combination of experiential workshops, viral attractions like the Leaf Pit, and year-round retail creates multiple revenue streams and repeat visitation that typical seasonal venues cannot match. Strong social media shareability drives organic acquisition.

Year One Milestones

  • Secure a 20+ acre site and complete venue build-out including workshop spaces, retail barn, and Leaf Pit
  • Attract 50,000 visitors in the first fall season through social media virality and local press coverage
  • Generate $1.5 million in combined revenue from admissions, workshops, retail, and events

Key Risks

  • Heavy seasonality: peak revenue concentrated in September through November, requiring creative off-season programming to maintain cash flow
  • High upfront capital for land, structures, and orchard establishment with a multi-year timeline to mature apple trees
  • Weather dependency: rain, early frost, or drought can significantly impact both crop yield and visitor turnout
