Explore the Mission

The Problem

Space Is Everywhere, Yet Nowhere to Experience

Children grow up dreaming about stars, planets, and astronauts. Adults marvel at rocket launches and Mars rovers. Yet for most people, the closest they can get to space is a small corner of a natural history museum or a once-in-a-lifetime trip to a space camp.

There is no dedicated, repeatable venue for space exploration. No place families can visit season after season, the way they return to zoos, aquariums, and botanical gardens. The fascination is universal; the access is not.

The Solution

A Space Experience Center Built for Repeat Visits

LunarLand is a standalone venue purpose-built for the wonder of space. Imagine walking through a simulated lunar surface, launching model rockets, watching a live planetarium show, and training like an astronaut, all in one afternoon.

With rotating seasonal exhibits and partnerships with NASA and private space companies, there is always a reason to come back. Memberships, school programs, and birthday parties ensure a steady revenue stream while building a community of young explorers.

Key Features

Four Pillars of the LunarLand Experience

Lunar Surface Play Zone

A simulated moon environment with low-gravity bounce areas, crater exploration, and interactive terrain where visitors walk, jump, and play on a recreated lunar landscape.

NASA Partnership Exhibits

Rotating displays featuring real mission artifacts, astronaut gear, and behind-the-scenes content from active space programs. New installations every quarter keep visitors returning.

STEM Workshops

Hands-on sessions where kids build model rockets, program rovers, learn about orbital mechanics, and watch immersive planetarium shows. Curriculum-aligned for school field trips.

VR Space Missions

Step inside a virtual spacecraft for astronaut training simulations, spacewalk experiences, and guided tours of the International Space Station. Premium upsell with high replay value.

We have zoos for land. Aquariums for sea. It is time for a place dedicated to the final frontier.

Business Plan

The Mission Briefing

Revenue Model

General admission tickets ($15 to $30 per person), annual membership passes, school group packages, birthday party and event rentals, a themed gift shop, and a space-themed snack bar. Premium VR experiences and simulated spacewalks provide high-margin upsell opportunities.

Go-to-Market Strategy

Launch a flagship location in a mid-size metro area with strong family demographics. Partner with local schools for field trip programs during the first semester. Validate unit economics over 18 months, then expand through franchising to additional metro areas.

Competitive Advantage

Unlike static museum exhibits, LunarLand is purpose-built for repeat visits with rotating content and seasonal events. The franchise model allows rapid geographic expansion, while partnerships with NASA and private space companies (SpaceX, Blue Origin) provide exclusive content no competitor can replicate. There is currently no franchise-scale space experience center in the market.

Year One Milestones

Launch annual membership program with 2,000 founding member families.

03

Key Risks

High upfront build-out costs for immersive exhibits and simulated environments.
Seasonal attendance fluctuations and competition from other family entertainment options.
Difficulty securing and maintaining NASA or space agency partnerships at scale.
