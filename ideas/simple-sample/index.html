Business Plan

The blueprint.

Revenue Model

Three streams working together: (1) consumer subscriptions at $1.99 to $3.99 per month, (2) brand partnerships where companies pay to place samples in curated boxes, and (3) affiliate commissions on full-size purchases made through the platform after subscribers discover products they love.

Go-to-Market Strategy

Launch with a single category (toothpaste) and a waitlist campaign on social media, leaning into the relatable frustration of never knowing if your toothpaste is actually the best. Partner with three to five mid-tier brands willing to supply samples. Scale to deodorant, soap, and shampoo within six months.

Competitive Advantage

Unlike Birchbox or Ipsy at $15 to $25 per month with random luxury items, Simple Sample is radically affordable and focused on everyday staples. The head-to-head comparison format is unique: every box is a structured shootout. Brands get higher-intent leads because subscribers are actively evaluating, not just collecting.

Year One Milestones

  • Launch toothpaste category with 5,000 subscribers and three brand partners within 90 days
  • Expand to four product categories (toothpaste, deodorant, soap, shampoo) by month six
  • Reach 25,000 active subscribers and sign 15 brand partnerships generating $200K in brand revenue

Key Risks

  • Shipping economics are tough at a $2 to $4 price point, requiring brand subsidies or high volume to break even
  • Large brands may prefer their own direct-to-consumer sampling programs over a third-party platform
  • Subscriber churn could be high once people find their preferred products and no longer need samples
