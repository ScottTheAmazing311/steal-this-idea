Why Families Love It

More than a box of toys. Block Party is a whole new way to play.

Monthly Delivery, Prepaid Returns

Every kit arrives with a prepaid return label. Shipping both ways is included in your membership. No extra fees, no trips to the post office.

Community Swap Network

Start a local toy swap with neighbors and friends. The app coordinates who buys what so everyone gets maximum variety for minimum spend.

Every returned kit goes through professional cleaning, sanitization, and a full piece count. Missing something? We replace it before it ships to the next family.

Keep What You Love

Fall in love with a build? Keep it at an exclusive member discount. No pressure, no obligation. Only hold onto the ones that earn a permanent spot on the shelf.

A Massive Market, Wide Open

Construction toys and puzzles are a multi-billion dollar category with almost no rental or subscription competition.

$9B+ in Lego Revenue Alone

Lego Group generates over $9 billion per year. That is just one brand in a construction toy category worth tens of billions globally.

Subscription Economy Booming

The toy subscription box market in the U.S. is estimated at $500M and growing fast as parents look for curated, convenient experiences.

Box-Shaped = Shippable

Unlike past toy rental failures (Toy Guru), Legos and puzzles come in uniform, stackable boxes. Warehousing and shipping costs stay predictable and low.

Community Creates Moats

The local swap feature builds network effects that pure e-commerce cannot replicate. Once a neighborhood is on Block Party, switching costs are high.

The Business Plan

A clear path from concept to community, built on predictable unit economics and two complementary revenue streams.

Revenue Model

  • Monthly subscriptions from $15 to $30 depending on kit tier and complexity
  • Buy-to-keep purchases at discounted member pricing
  • Free community swap app monetized through Amazon affiliate links on every toy purchase
  • Premium family plans with access to exclusive or early-release kits

Go-to-Market Strategy

Launch with a curated catalog of 200 popular Lego sets and 100 puzzles. Target parent communities on Instagram and Facebook with ads showcasing the full unboxing-to-return cycle. Seed the community swap feature in 10 family-dense neighborhoods to validate the local model before scaling nationwide.

Competitive Advantage

Previous toy rental services failed because toys come in wildly different shapes and sizes, making logistics a nightmare. Block Party focuses exclusively on box-shaped, standardized items: Legos, puzzles, and tabletop games. This makes warehousing simple and shipping costs predictable. The community swap layer adds local network effects that pure rental services cannot match.

Year One Milestones

  • Reach 2,000 active subscribers and 50 community swap groups across 10 metro areas
  • Achieve a kit return rate above 90% with fewer than 5% of kits needing piece replacements
  • Launch partnerships with two major toy brands for exclusive or early-access kits

Key Risks

  • Lost or damaged pieces leading to high replacement costs and inventory shrinkage over time
  • Potential Lego Group licensing or distribution restrictions on rental and resale of their products
  • Low retention if families feel the monthly fee does not justify the value compared to buying outright

Startup Cost Estimate

Estimated $80,000 to $200,000 to reach MVP. Core costs include initial toy inventory (200+ kits), warehouse setup, cleaning and inspection equipment, app development for the community swap feature, and marketing for launch in the first 10 neighborhoods.
