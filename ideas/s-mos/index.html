S'Mos

Gather around. Reconnect over fire.

A nostalgia-driven social lounge built around communal fire pits, craft s'mores, and cocktails. The warmth of a campfire gathering, right in the heart of the city.

Reserve a Fire Pit
The Problem

We forgot how to be together.

Urban adults crave real social connection but keep ending up at the same loud bars and overpriced restaurants. Everyone sits across a table staring at their phones.

The campfire, one of humanity's oldest gathering rituals, is completely absent from city life. Nostalgia for simpler times is everywhere, yet nobody has built a real experience around it.

  • 87% of millennials say they value experiences over material goods
  • Screen time is up, but meaningful in-person connection is down
  • Experiential dining is booming, but most concepts feel gimmicky
  • The nostalgia economy is worth billions, yet campfire culture has zero commercial presence in cities
The Solution

Fire pits. Craft s'mores. No phones allowed.

S'Mos is a social lounge centered around communal gas-powered fire pits where guests build gourmet s'mores, sip craft cocktails, and actually talk to each other.

A strict no-devices policy turns every visit into something rare: genuine human presence. The smell of toasted marshmallow, the crackle of flame, the warmth on your hands. It is simple. It is powerful. People remember it.

Communal Fire Pits

Indoor and patio gas-powered fire pits seating 4-12 guests each. Walk in solo or reserve a private pit for your group.

Gourmet S'mores Bar

30+ premium toppings, artisan marshmallows, craft chocolates, and seasonal specials. Build your own or try the chef's creations.

Device-Free Zone

Lock your phone in a pouch at the door. Reclaim your attention. This is the experience people never knew they needed.

What We Serve

More than s'mores. It is a ritual.

01

Build-Your-Own S'mores Bar

Choose your graham (classic, cinnamon, chocolate), your marshmallow (vanilla bean, salted caramel, lavender), and your chocolate. Then toast it yourself over a real flame. Seasonal menus keep regulars coming back.

02

Fireside Cocktails and Boozy Hot Chocolate

Signature drinks designed to complement the campfire vibe. Smoked old fashioneds, spiked ciders, and a hot chocolate menu that rivals any specialty coffee shop in town.

03

Private Fire Pit Reservations

Book a pit for date night, a birthday celebration, or a team outing. Each private pit comes with a dedicated topping spread, a drink package, and blankets for the full outdoor lounge experience.

04

The No-Phone Promise

Guests lock their devices in custom pouches at the entrance. It sounds extreme. It is the single most talked-about feature. People leave saying it was the best evening they have had in years.

Business Plan

Low food cost. High-margin drinks. Unforgettable experience.

Revenue Model

Per-seat cover charge ($15-25) includes a base s'mores kit. Premium toppings and signature cocktails drive high-margin upsells. Private fire pit reservations run $150-300 per session. Corporate event packages and branded merchandise (marshmallow kits, mugs, candles) add recurring revenue streams both on-site and online.

Go-to-Market Strategy

Open the first location in a high-foot-traffic urban neighborhood like Austin, Nashville, or Denver. Build buzz with a pop-up series before committing to a permanent space. Partner with local influencers and corporate event planners from day one. Use the device-free policy as a PR hook to land earned media coverage.

Competitive Advantage

S'Mos is not a restaurant that serves s'mores as a menu item. It is an experience brand built on nostalgia, sensory design, and social connection. Low food costs (marshmallows, chocolate, graham crackers) paired with high-margin drinks create strong unit economics. The device-free policy generates organic word-of-mouth that money cannot buy.

Year One Milestones

  • Open flagship location and achieve $120K+ monthly revenue within 6 months
  • Host 200+ corporate and private events in the first year
  • Build a waitlist of 5,000+ for the second location and explore franchising

Key Risks

  • Seasonal demand may skew toward fall and winter, requiring creative warm-weather programming
  • Novelty factor could fade without evolving menus, themed events, and collaborations
  • Fire safety regulations and indoor ventilation requirements add complexity to buildout
