The Problem

Car Ownership Is a Mess of Forgotten Tasks

Your car needs constant attention, but nobody tells you when or what. Oil changes slip past their due date. Registrations expire. Recall notices arrive disguised as junk mail. Insurance rates quietly climb while better deals go unnoticed.

  • Missed oil changes, forgotten tire rotations, expired registrations
  • Recall notices buried in physical mail that never get opened
  • Overpaying on insurance because comparing rates is a chore
  • Manufacturer apps that are "comically bad" and never send push notifications

The Solution

One App That Knows Your Car Better Than You Do

GloveBox replaces every sticker on your windshield, every postcard in your mailbox, and every forgotten login to a manufacturer portal. Enter your vehicle once. We handle the rest.

  • Scan your registration and insurance cards; they are always one tap away
  • Smart reminders for every maintenance milestone, customized to your car
  • Instant recall alerts pushed to your phone, not buried in junk mail
  • Compare insurance rates and switch policies without leaving the app

Market Opportunity

A Massive Market With No Central Hub

Every car owner in America needs maintenance, insurance, and document management. Yet no single app unifies these services today. Manufacturer apps are fragmented and poorly built. Insurance apps only care about their own policies. Parts retailers live on separate platforms entirely.

GloveBox sits at the center of this ecosystem, connecting owners to service providers through a single, brand-agnostic interface. The target: everyday drivers aged 22 to 55 who are not car enthusiasts but still need their vehicles to run smoothly.

Business Plan

How GloveBox Makes Money and Grows

A real business model built on affiliate revenue, strategic partnerships, and a freemium upgrade path.

Revenue Model

Affiliate commissions from insurance providers, tire shops, oil change chains, and parts retailers. Premium subscription tier for driving analytics, multi-vehicle management, and priority partner discounts. The insurance marketplace takes a cut of each policy switch.

Go-to-Market Strategy

Launch a free MVP with document scanning, car valuation, and basic maintenance reminders. Partner with one national chain (Jiffy Lube or Discount Tire) to push service records into the app automatically. Grow through word of mouth and content marketing targeting the universal pain of forgotten oil changes and expired registrations.

Competitive Advantage

No app unifies document storage, maintenance tracking, insurance comparison, recall alerts, parts ordering, and resale valuation in one place. Manufacturer apps are siloed and poorly built. GloveBox is brand-agnostic and creates a network effect as service providers push data directly into the platform.

Year One Milestones

  • Launch iOS and Android apps with document storage, maintenance reminders, and car valuation
  • Secure two national service chain partnerships for integrated service records
  • Reach 100,000 active users and generate first revenue through insurance referrals

Key Risks

  • Established players like Progressive, Geico, or Apple CarPlay could add similar features using their existing user bases
  • Convincing fragmented service providers to integrate requires significant business development effort
  • User acquisition costs may be high in the crowded auto services advertising market
