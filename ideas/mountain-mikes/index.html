The Problem

Camping should be simple.
It is anything but.

The best camping experiences require serious gear: an RV or camper ($20K to $50K), a truck to tow it ($25K+), and a garage full of equipment. For most people, that price tag turns a weekend in the woods into a pipe dream.

  • RVs and campers cost $20,000 to $50,000, plus a capable tow vehicle
  • RV parks are crowded, often uninspiring, and feel like parking lots
  • Hauling gear across the country burns fuel, time, and patience
  • Forgotten supplies mean frustrating trips back to town mid-adventure

The Solution

Your basecamp is already waiting.

Mountain Mike's stations a fleet of RVs and campers at the doorstep of national parks and top camping destinations. Drive up in any car. Pick your rig. Grab a gear package. We deliver you to a pre-scouted premium campsite.

  1. 1 Pull up in any vehicle. Drive your sedan, your Prius, whatever you have. No truck needed, no trailer hitch required.
  2. 2 Choose your setup. Pick from a fleet of RVs and campers, then add a gear package: basic firewood and s'mores, or the full adventure bundle with fishing poles and sleeping bags.
  3. 3 Head to your pre-scouted site. Lakeside, deep in the pines, or near the mountains. We know all the best spots, and we will drive you there if you like.
  4. 4 End with hot showers and ice cream. When you are done, return the rig, rinse off, and grab a double scoop before heading home.

Business Plan

Built for the wild. Backed by numbers.

Revenue Model

Per-trip RV and camper rentals ($150 to $400 per night), tiered add-on gear packages ($50 to $200), guided experience upsells (fly fishing, hiking tours), on-site retail (camp store, ice cream shop, hot showers), and franchise fees from new locations.

Go-to-Market Strategy

Launch a flagship location adjacent to one high-traffic national park (Yellowstone or Glacier) with 8 to 12 RVs. Build brand awareness through outdoor influencer partnerships, targeted search ads for camping and national park queries, and affiliate deals with travel booking platforms. Validate unit economics over two seasons before franchising.

Competitive Advantage

Unlike traditional RV rental companies, Mountain Mike's positions inventory at the destination. This eliminates the need for towing capability, dramatically reduces vehicle wear, and creates a curated, premium experience (pre-scouted sites, gear bundles, guided tours) that pure rental companies cannot replicate.

Year One Milestones

  • Open flagship with 10 RVs, achieve 70% occupancy during peak season (May through September)
  • Generate $500K in gross revenue with positive unit economics by end of first season
  • Secure letters of intent from 3 franchise candidates for Year 2 expansion

Key Risks

  • Seasonality limits revenue to 5 to 7 months per year in many regions, requiring strong peak-season margins
  • Securing permits and land leases near national parks involves complex government approval processes
  • High upfront capital for RV fleet and site development before revenue begins
