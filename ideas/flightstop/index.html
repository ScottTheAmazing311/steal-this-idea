Airport Rental Kiosks

FlightStop

Rent gear, upgrade your flight.

FlightStop places rental kiosks in airports so travelers can grab entertainment, comfort essentials, baby gear, and weather apparel on the spot. No more dead devices, overstuffed bags, or unpredictable amenities.

The Problem

Flying Shouldn't Be This Painful

Every traveler knows the frustration. You arrive at your gate, settle into your seat, and realize you are completely unprepared for the next few hours.

Unpredictable Amenities

Will there be a power outlet? A screen? A USB port? You have no idea until you are already buckled in at 30,000 feet.

Suitcase Space is Sacred

Every square inch of your luggage is accounted for. Bulky travel pillows, blankets, and entertainment devices rarely make the cut.

Traveling with Kids is a Nightmare

Car seats, strollers, and pack-and-plays are heavy, awkward, and miserable to haul through terminals.

Weather Surprises

You fly into an unexpected snowstorm or downpour with nothing but a t-shirt. Sound familiar?

The Solution

Your Airport Gear Concierge

FlightStop kiosks sit right in the terminal, stocked with everything you need for a better flight. Rent what you want on the way in, return it at your destination. It is that simple.

Think of it as a vending machine meets a gear library. Entertainment, comfort, baby essentials, weather gear, and even bundled snacks at fair prices.

What We Offer

Everything You Need, Right at the Gate

Four categories of rentals designed to cover every kind of traveler, from the solo business flyer to the family heading to grandma's house.

Entertainment and Tech

Preloaded iPads with movies and shows, Kindles stocked with bestsellers, Nintendo Switches with top games, and portable chargers. Your dead battery is no longer a problem.

Comfort and Relaxation

Premium noise-cancelling headphones, memory foam travel pillows, cozy blankets, and eye masks. The Relaxation Bundle turns any middle seat into first class.

Baby and Toddler Gear

Rent car seats, strollers, and pack-and-plays at your destination instead of lugging them through TSA. Pick up when you land, return before you fly home.

Weather-Ready Apparel

Landing in a surprise snowstorm? Grab a coat, gloves, or rain poncho at the kiosk. Travel light knowing FlightStop has you covered at the other end.

Business Plan

Built to Scale, Terminal by Terminal

A practical roadmap for turning FlightStop from concept to a nationwide airport presence.

Revenue Model

Rental Fees and Premium Bundles

Per-item and bundled rental fees charged at the kiosk. The "Relaxation Bundle" (blanket, pillow, headphones) runs $60, while single items like a Nintendo Switch rent for $30 to $50 per flight. Snack and drink add-ons at fair prices boost average order value. Revenue scales linearly with each new kiosk location.

Go-to-Market Strategy

Two Airports, Then the World

Launch at two high-traffic US airports, prioritizing pre-security kiosk space where rent is lower and foot traffic is guaranteed. Study the CLEAR rollout playbook for expansion sequencing. Build relationships with airport authorities early, prove unit economics at the first two locations, then franchise the model.

Competitive Advantage

First Mover in an Empty Space

The closest predecessor was a single-category portable DVD player rental that no longer exists. No one is offering a comprehensive, multi-category rental experience at airports. FlightStop combines entertainment, comfort, baby gear, and weather apparel into one brand, creating a defensible position that would be difficult to replicate quickly.

Key Risks
  • Airport real estate is expensive and governed by complex leasing agreements that take time to negotiate
  • Device theft or damage could erode margins, even with credit card holds as a safeguard
  • Licensing restrictions on preloaded digital content (movies, books, games) may limit the entertainment category
Year One Milestones
Q1 - Q2

Launch kiosks in two major US airports with full entertainment and comfort inventory. Validate pricing, measure daily rental volume, and refine the bundle strategy.

Q3

Reach 5,000 rentals per month across both locations. Optimize kiosk restocking logistics and begin collecting customer feedback data for expansion planning.

Q4

Introduce baby gear and weather apparel categories. Launch bundled pricing tiers and begin conversations with three additional airport authorities for Year Two expansion.
