FIND YOUR SPOT
THE PROBLEM

Parking in America Is Broken

You are heading to the big game. You leave early. You drive past the stadium once, twice, three times. Every lot is full, cash-only, or charging absurd prices. You end up a mile away, walking in the rain, already frustrated before kickoff.

Meanwhile, homeowners with empty driveways and businesses with vacant lots sit just a block away, watching the chaos with no way to help (or profit).

  • Drivers waste 17+ hours per year searching for parking
  • Cash-only lots with no transparency or guarantees
  • Millions of residential and commercial spots sit empty during peak events
  • Existing parking companies are archaic, fragmented, and user-hostile
THE SOLUTION

Every Driveway Becomes a Parking Spot

Park Shark creates a two-sided marketplace that connects drivers with available parking. Homeowners list their driveways, businesses open their after-hours lots, and apartment dwellers rent out spots while they are away.

Drivers search, reserve, and pay in advance. Clear directions, transparent pricing, and real reviews. No surprises.

BUSINESS PLAN

The Roadmap to Disrupting Parking

REVENUE MODEL

Park Shark takes a 15-20% service fee on every transaction. Additional revenue streams include premium listings for commercial lot operators, surge pricing commissions during peak events, and upsell fees on value-add services like car washes and EV charging.

GO-TO-MARKET STRATEGY

Launch city by city, starting with neighborhoods adjacent to major stadiums and arenas. Partner with fan communities and tailgate groups for grassroots adoption. Onboard nearby businesses and homeowners with guaranteed minimum payouts for the first season.

COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGE

Park Shark targets the overlooked supply of residential driveways, business lots, and apartment guest spots that traditional parking companies ignore entirely. By combining peer-to-peer listings with commercial lot digitization, it builds the largest, most flexible parking inventory in any city.

YEAR ONE MILESTONES

  • Launch in 3 cities with 500+ active host spots per city
  • Process 50,000 reservations in the first 12 months
  • Achieve a 4.5+ average host and driver satisfaction rating

KEY RISKS

  • Liability and insurance concerns if vehicles are damaged on private property
  • Local zoning regulations or HOA rules that restrict commercial use of residential driveways
  • Incumbent parking companies lobbying against peer-to-peer competition
