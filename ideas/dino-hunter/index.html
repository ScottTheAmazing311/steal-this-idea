The Problem

Adventure Entertainment Is Stuck in the Past

Monster truck rallies are declining. You sit in the stands and watch. Go-kart tracks feel the same as they did 30 years ago. Paintball arenas have barely changed since the 1990s.

People crave participatory thrills, not passive spectating. They want to be the hero of their own story, not a face in the crowd. Yet the entertainment industry keeps recycling the same tired formats.

The Solution

You Drive. You Shoot. You Hunt.

Dino Hunter puts your group of four inside a real monster truck on an outdoor course filled with life-sized dinosaur targets. One person drives. Three ride shotgun with paintball guns. You compete against other teams to rack up the highest score in a 20-30 minute session that feels like stepping into Jurassic Park.

No special license required. No experience necessary. Just show up, gear up, and hunt. The courses rotate monthly with new challenges, and seasonal themes keep it fresh: Zombie Hunter in October, Alien Hunter in summer. Every visit is a new adventure.

The Experience

Four Ways to Get Your Adrenaline Fix

Real Monster Trucks

Drive actual full-sized monster trucks across rugged outdoor terrain. No special license needed. Professional safety briefing gets you road-ready in minutes.

Competitive Paintball Scoring

Three gunners per truck, armed with paintball markers, compete to tag as many dinosaur targets as possible. Real-time scoring and leaderboards fuel the rivalry.

Rotating Themed Courses

New course layouts drop monthly. Seasonal themes keep you coming back: Zombie Hunter for Halloween, Alien Hunter in summer, Arctic Predator in winter.

Group and Corporate Packages

Book private sessions for bachelor parties, birthdays, or corporate team-building. Spectator areas let the whole crew watch the carnage unfold live.

The Business Plan

Built to Scale, Designed to Franchise

Revenue Model

Per-group session pricing at $150-250 for a team of four (20-30 minute run). Premium packages include extended sessions, exclusive arena bookings, and VIP spectator experiences. Additional revenue from corporate event packages, seasonal themed events, merchandise, and photo/video packages.

Go-to-Market Strategy

Launch a flagship location in a suburban market with affordable land and strong family demographics. Generate organic buzz through social media video content; monster trucks and paintball are inherently viral. Partner with corporate event planners, local tourism boards, and group activity platforms for initial bookings.

Competitive Advantage

No direct competitor combines monster truck driving with paintball gaming in a themed outdoor arena. The concept leverages underutilized rural land, requires no special licensing for participants, and has built-in franchise scalability since the format is portable and repeatable across locations. High barriers to entry from capital requirements protect against fast followers.

Year One Milestones

  • Open flagship location and achieve 5,000 bookings in the first 12 months
  • Generate $750K+ in revenue with 60%+ gross margins on session pricing
  • Secure letters of intent for 3 franchise locations based on flagship success

Startup Cost Estimate

$500K - $1.2M

Covers monster truck fleet (4-6 vehicles), land lease or purchase, course construction, dinosaur props and targets, paintball equipment, safety infrastructure, insurance, and initial marketing.

Key Risks

  • High upfront capital for monster trucks, land, and dinosaur props/targets
  • Liability and insurance costs for letting untrained drivers operate large vehicles
  • Seasonal limitations in cold-weather markets could reduce operating months
