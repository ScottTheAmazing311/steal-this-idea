The Problem

Retreats are broken.

When life throws you a curveball, you deserve more than a trust fall and a workbook.

Corporate Cliches

Ropes courses, icebreakers, and mandatory "synergy" sessions. Most people think of retreats as something their HR department forces on them. The word itself has been co-opted by boardrooms.

Fragmented and Forgettable

The few personal retreats that exist are hyper-regional, poorly marketed, and lack any real programming. No recognizable brands. No consistent quality. Just a cabin and a vague promise.

Vacations Without Direction

You can book a beach trip or a European tour, but neither addresses what you actually need: space to process a loss, tools to reconnect with a partner, or permission to unplug and reset.

The Solution

Vacations that actually change you.

Retreatful pairs world-class destinations with therapist-led programming built around life's biggest moments.

Every Retreatful retreat is five days in a stunning, remote location. Small groups of 15 people connect through guided conversations, shared activities, and intentional downtime. No workbooks. No lectures. Just real growth set against the backdrop of a beautiful trip you would have wanted to take anyway.

5-Day Immersive Format

Strangers becoming confidants in a curated, safe environment.

Real professionals guiding every conversation and activity.

How It Works

Built for transformation.

Every element is designed to help you show up, open up, and leave lighter than you arrived.

Therapist-Led Programming

Each retreat is guided by licensed professionals specializing in the specific life transition at hand: grief, divorce recovery, couples renewal, new parenthood, creativity, or digital detox.

Stunning Remote Locations

Big Sur. The Redwoods. Moab. The Smoky Mountains. Every retreat takes place in a breathtaking, remote location paired with curated local activities like kayaking, wine tasting, and fireside conversations.

Intimate Group Connection

Groups of just 15 people create genuine bonds with others facing similar challenges. Breakout sessions, partner activities, and group fireside chats all foster deep, meaningful relationships.

Scalable Franchise Model

Licensed therapists and counselors can launch their own Retreatful retreats with full brand support, marketing resources, proven playbooks, and booking infrastructure. One brand, many voices.

The Opportunity

A massive market, waiting.

Wellness tourism and mental health services are converging into one of the fastest growing categories in travel.

Adults aged 25 to 50 with mid-to-upper incomes are prioritizing experiences over possessions, mental health over hustle culture, and intentional travel over passive vacations. This generation spends more on wellness, therapy, and personal development than any before it.

Yet no single brand owns the "therapeutic travel retreat" category. The existing landscape is fragmented: regional operators with poor websites, inconsistent quality, and zero brand recognition. Retreatful steps into a wide open space with a scalable model and a clear value proposition.

The Business Plan

From first retreat to franchise.

Revenue Model

Per-person retreat fees ranging from $1,000 to $2,500 for a 5-day experience. At 15 guests per retreat and 4 retreats per month per location, a single location generates roughly $60K to $150K monthly. Additional revenue from franchise licensing fees and group-rate activity margins.

Go-to-Market Strategy

Launch with three flagship retreats run hands-on by the founding team: couples in Big Sur, grief in the Redwoods, digital detox in Moab. Market through targeted digital ads mirroring existing life-event indicators (divorce attorneys, grief resources, parenting blogs) and therapist referral partnerships.

Competitive Advantage

No established brand currently owns this category. Existing retreats are fragmented, poorly marketed, and hyper-regional. Retreatful combines vacation-quality travel with structured therapeutic programming under one recognizable brand, then scales through a franchise model that gives therapists proven playbooks.

Year One Milestones

  • Execute 12 flagship retreats across 3 locations with 80%+ occupancy and strong NPS scores
  • Onboard 5 licensed therapist-operators into the franchise model with full brand and marketing support
  • Build a waitlist of 500+ interested participants through digital marketing and therapist referral channels

Key Risks

  • Liability and regulatory complexity around combining therapeutic services with travel across state lines
  • High upfront cost of securing locations, therapists, and programming before proving demand
  • Overcoming the negative connotation of "retreat" and differentiating from corporate or yoga-cliche associations

Startup Cost Estimate

Getting from concept to first three operational retreats with marketing, therapist contracts, location deposits, website, and working capital.
