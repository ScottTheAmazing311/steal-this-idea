Estimated Addressable Market

Sports fans aged 21 to 45 who already own smart home devices are the primary audience. These are people who invest in their viewing experience: large TVs, sound systems, team merchandise, streaming subscriptions. They want their homes to feel like a stadium, not a living room.

The secondary market includes esports enthusiasts, sports bars looking to differentiate, and the growing "man cave" culture. Globally, soccer fandom offers enormous scale. Even capturing just 1% of the overlap between smart home owners and active sports fans yields a market well north of $500M.

BUSINESS PLAN

THE PLAYBOOK

REVENUE MODEL

Freemium app subscription: a free tier supporting one team with basic color changes, and a $9.99/month Pro tier that unlocks unlimited teams, custom celebrations, soundbar integration, and esports support. Hardware starter bundles sold through the website at $149 to $399. Professional installation packages at $299 to $799 for the full fan cave setup. B2B licensing for sports bars and entertainment venues rounds out the revenue streams.

GO-TO-MARKET STRATEGY

Launch with iOS and Android apps supporting Philips Hue and the NFL/NBA during the fall sports season, targeting home automation subreddits and sports fan communities where demand is already proven through DIY posts. Partner with smart lighting brands for co-branded bundles and run targeted campaigns during NFL kickoff and March Madness to capture fans at peak engagement. Leverage user-generated content of lit-up rooms on social media to drive organic growth.

COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGE

No productized solution currently exists for sports-triggered home automation. DIY setups require hours of configuration across Home Assistant, IFTTT, and custom scripts. HomeTeam is the first company to package this into a consumer-friendly app with plug-and-play simplicity. First-mover advantage in a space where fan passion and smart home adoption are both accelerating creates a strong defensible position.
