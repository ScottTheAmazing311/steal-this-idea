THE PROBLEM
The Dead Zone Between Checkout and Takeoff

Your hotel checkout is at 11 AM. Your flight leaves at 3 PM. You have four hours in an incredible city, but you are stuck dragging a suitcase down crowded sidewalks or paying for an Uber just to stash your bags somewhere inconvenient.

Most travelers either sit in a hotel lobby scrolling their phone, haul luggage through tourist areas, or waste money on rides back and forth to wherever they left their stuff. It is the most common frustration in travel, and nobody has built a real solution for it.

THE SOLUTION
Stash. Explore. Shuttle. Fly.

Drop Stop is a network of secure, smart locker stations placed at the heart of every major tourist district. Combined with a frequent airport shuttle, it turns dead travel time into prime exploration time.

  1. Check out of your hotel and head to the nearest Drop Stop, right in the middle of the action.
  2. Scan in, choose a locker, and stash your bags in seconds. Everything is digital, secure, and insured.
  3. Explore the city completely hands-free. Restaurants, bars, attractions: all within walking distance.
  4. When it is time to fly, grab your bags and hop on the Drop Stop airport shuttle, departing every 30 minutes.
KEY FEATURES
Built for the Way You Travel

Smart Self-Service Lockers

Digital screens handle payment and access codes. Multiple sizes accommodate everything from backpacks to oversized checked bags. No staff interaction required.

Airport Shuttle Every 30 Min

Frequent, reliable shuttle service runs directly from every Drop Stop to the airport. Skip the Uber surge pricing and ride stress-free.

Prime Tourist Locations

Every Drop Stop is placed at the epicenter of tourist activity. Broadway in Nashville, Pike Place in Seattle, the French Quarter in New Orleans. Right where you want to be.

Hotel Shuttle Partnerships

Partner hotels shuttle guests directly to Drop Stop locations. Hotels get a perk to offer guests. Travelers get a seamless experience from checkout to exploration.

THE MARKET
A Massive, Untapped Traveler Need

The US travel and tourism industry generates over $1.1 trillion annually. With 2.3 billion domestic person-trips each year and an estimated 30% of travelers experiencing the checkout-to-flight gap, the addressable market is enormous.

The primary audience is frequent business travelers who experience this pain point repeatedly. The secondary market includes leisure travelers, tour groups, and corporate travel departments. As cities continue to invest in walkable downtown districts, the demand for hands-free urban exploration only grows.

THE BUSINESS PLAN
From Parking Lot to Profit

Revenue Model

A hybrid membership and per-use pricing structure. Monthly members pay $10/month for access to all Drop Stop locations nationwide. One-time users pay $20-25 per use. The airport shuttle is bundled in. Additional revenue streams include advertising partnerships with nearby restaurants and attractions, plus referral commissions from partner hotels.

Go-to-Market Strategy

Launch with a single high-traffic pilot location in Nashville, positioned on or near Broadway. Partner with 2-3 nearby hotels (Hilton, Marriott, etc.) to offer shuttle service from their lobby to the Drop Stop. Validate demand and unit economics with real data before expanding to the next 5 cities.

Competitive Advantage

Unlike the informal practice of checking bags at a random hotel (which most people find awkward or do not know about), Drop Stop is a branded, trusted, always-available service located exactly where travelers want to be. The combination of secure storage plus airport shuttle creates a value proposition no hotel bell desk can match.

Year One Milestones

  • Launch pilot location in Nashville with 50+ locker capacity and airport shuttle route
  • Reach 500 monthly members and 2,000 one-time users within the first year
  • Secure partnership agreements with 5+ hotels for the shuttle referral program

Startup Cost Estimate

Smart luggage lockers run approximately $2,000 per unit. Combined with shuttle leasing, initial marketing, and real estate negotiation, the total pilot launch cost is estimated at:

$15,000 - $40,000

Key Risks

  • Hotels already offer free bag check, which limits willingness to pay for a separate service
  • Securing prime real estate in tourist corridors may require creative partnership deals rather than traditional leases
  • Liability concerns around lost, stolen, or damaged luggage in self-service locker facilities
