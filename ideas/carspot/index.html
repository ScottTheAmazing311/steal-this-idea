The Problem

Dealerships are Broken
by Design

Traditional car dealerships sit on 20,000 to 100,000 square feet of asphalt, baking hundreds of vehicles under the sun. They pay for land, security, washing, storm repairs, and armies of salespeople. All that overhead gets baked right into the sticker price of every car on the lot.


Meanwhile, today's buyers do their research online long before setting foot on a lot. They already know the make, model, and color they want. The giant lot full of cars? It is mostly theater, and you are paying for the show.

The Solution

Smaller Showrooms.
Smarter Sales.

CarSpot flips the dealership model on its head. Instead of a sprawling lot off the freeway, we place compact showrooms in high-traffic locations: malls, downtown corridors, retail districts. Each showroom carries just two of every model for test drives.

All inventory lives in one central hub.

After your test drive, you configure your perfect car and purchase on the spot. Within 48 hours, your vehicle is delivered straight to your driveway from our climate-controlled distribution center. No haggling with ten different salespeople. No driving across town to find the right color. No inflated prices to cover empty acres of asphalt.

The Opportunity

A $900B Market,
Ripe for Disruption

The US new car market generates roughly $900 billion in annual revenue. Dealerships have operated the same way for decades: massive lots, aggressive sales floors, and overhead costs that inflate every sticker price.


Today's car buyers are 25 to 50 years old, digitally savvy, and do extensive online research before ever visiting a lot. They value convenience, transparency, and fair pricing over the "car lot experience." CarSpot meets them where they already are.


Even capturing just 0.5% of this market in five years represents $4.5 billion in sales, with margins improved by the dramatically lower-cost operating model.

The Business Plan

From Concept to Roadworthy

Revenue Model

Per-vehicle sales margins remain competitive despite lower consumer pricing because overhead is drastically reduced. Additional revenue from financing partnerships, trade-in processing fees, and extended warranty packages. The showroom concept can also be licensed to existing dealership groups as a franchise.

Go-to-Market Strategy

Partner with an established dealership group to pilot the concept with a single brand in one metro area. Open two to three compact showrooms in high-traffic retail locations while operating one shared distribution center. Use pilot data to prove unit economics before expanding.

Competitive Advantage

By decoupling test drives from inventory storage, CarSpot places showrooms in premium retail locations that traditional dealerships could never afford. Centralized inventory eliminates the costly shuffle of transferring cars between lots and reduces damage, theft, and weather exposure.

Year One Milestones

  • Secure a dealership partnership and open the first three compact showrooms in one metro area
  • Achieve 500 vehicle sales with a measurable cost-per-sale reduction versus traditional lots
  • Demonstrate a net promoter score above 70 from buyers using the new experience

Key Risks

  • Franchise laws in many US states restrict how new cars can be sold and may require navigating complex dealer agreement regulations
  • Established dealership networks have deep political connections and may lobby against or resist the model
  • Some customers may still want to see exact color and trim options in person before committing
